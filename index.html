Who I am


When I was a kid I loved unpicking knots. Unravelling particularly complex ones always needed an action plan:

  1. understand how the knot works/which parts to pull

  2. decide on the best place to start

  3. plan out each subsequent move in the most optimal order

This micro-problem-solving was a precursor for my love of more complex problems today. Whether that’s through programming contests, video game puzzles, or finding the most likely spot for a code-breaking typo, the challenge of a difficult problem never daunts me—in fact, it’s what spurs me forwards
