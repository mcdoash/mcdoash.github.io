Who I am


When I was a kid, I loved unpicking knots. Unravelling particularly complex ones always needed an action plan:

  1. understand how the knot works/which parts to pull

  2. decide on the best place to start

  3. plan out each subsequent move in the most optimal order

This micro-problem-solving was a precursor for my love of more complex problems today. Whether that’s through programming contests, video game puzzles, or finding the most likely spot for a code-breaking typo, the challenge of a difficult problem never daunts me—in fact, it’s what spurs me forwards.

Central Programming Club


The development of my school's first computer programming club has truly been a labor of love. Our first meeting saw an astounding 50 people, and we have been holding a steady count of about 20 students ever since (the rest we lost to the demands of homework). I have put in over 300 hours thus far, from building and maintaining our website to planning and leading club activities. I have tackled projects such as an introduction to code for beginners, a Turtle Graphics Tutorial, and even helping to plan our own programming contest. What I am most proud of is facing my fear of public speaking to help bring together people with a passion for code and introduce it to others who would like to learn.

FutureWorks


FutureWorks is a Junior Achievement company that produces the London’s Little Picassos magazine filled with art from London high school students. Besides having my art featured in the book, I am also developing their website (it is still currently in-progress). I was able to extend my web development knowledge greatly; it was my first time working with jQuery and really getting into CSS animations. This allowed me to add a lot more interactive elements, creating a better user experience. Building this website has taught me how to work with the company in order to meet their goals and match their overall brand.
