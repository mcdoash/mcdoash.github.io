Who I am


When I was a kid, I loved unpicking knots. Unravelling particularly complex ones always needed an action plan:

  1. understand how the knot works/which parts to pull

  2. decide on the best place to start

  3. plan out each subsequent move in the most optimal order

This micro-problem-solving was a precursor for my love of more complex problems today. Whether that’s through programming contests, video game puzzles, or finding the most likely spot for a code-breaking typo, the challenge of a difficult problem never daunts me—in fact, it’s what spurs me forwards.

Central Programming Club


The development of my school's first computer programming club has truly been a labor of love. Our first meeting saw an astounding 50 people, and we've been holding a steady count of about 20 students ever since (the rest we lost to the demands of homework). I’ve committed over 300 hours thus far, from building and maintaining our website to planning and leading club activities. I've tackled projects such as an introduction to code for beginners, a Turtle Graphics Tutorial, and even helping to plan our own programming contest. What I'm most proud of, however, would be facing my fear of public speaking to help bring together people with a passion for code and introduce it others who'd like to learn.
